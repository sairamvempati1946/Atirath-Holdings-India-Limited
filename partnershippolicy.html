Partnership Policy

Terms and conditions for farmer partnerships, vendor partnerships, and strategic alliances with Atirath Holdings India Limited

Last Updated: January 1, 2025

1. Introduction

At Atirath Holdings India Limited, we believe in building long-term, mutually beneficial partnerships. This Partnership Policy outlines the terms, conditions, and expectations for all partners - including farmers, vendors, technology partners, and strategic allies - who wish to collaborate with us in our mission to build a greener India.

This policy applies to all partnership arrangements entered into with Atirath Holdings India Limited and supersedes any previous verbal or written agreements unless explicitly stated.

2. Types of Partnerships

Farmer Partnership

For farmers cultivating Napier Grass and supplying biomass for CBG production. Includes guaranteed buyback, training support, and technical assistance.

Vendor Partnership

For suppliers of equipment, technology, logistics, and services related to CBG plant operations and supply chain management.

Technology Partnership

For technology providers offering innovative solutions in biogas purification, automation, IoT, and renewable energy systems.

Strategic Alliance

For institutional partners, financial institutions, research organizations, and government bodies working towards common sustainability goals.

3. Eligibility for Partnership

To be eligible for partnership with Atirath Holdings, applicants must meet the following criteria:

  • Be a legally registered entity or individual with valid identity proof
  • Have relevant experience or capability in the proposed partnership area
  • Demonstrate commitment to quality, integrity, and timely delivery
  • Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards
  • Share our commitment to environmental sustainability and ethical business practices
  • Have no history of fraudulent or illegal activities

4. Partner Rights and Obligations

4.1 Rights of Partners

  • Right to fair and transparent treatment
  • Right to timely payments as per agreement terms
  • Right to access relevant information and support
  • Right to raise concerns and seek resolution
  • Right to terminate partnership as per agreement terms

4.2 Obligations of Partners

  • Deliver products/services as per agreed quality standards
  • Maintain confidentiality of business information
  • Comply with all safety and environmental regulations
  • Provide accurate documentation and invoices
  • Promptly communicate any issues or delays
  • Maintain required licenses and certifications

5. Atirath Holdings Commitments

As your partner, Atirath Holdings commits to:

  • Providing clear specifications and requirements for all engagements
  • Ensuring timely payments as per agreed terms
  • Offering technical support and training where applicable
  • Maintaining transparent communication channels
  • Respecting partner confidentiality and intellectual property
  • Providing fair dispute resolution mechanisms
  • Creating opportunities for mutual growth and development

6. Partnership Enrollment Process

The partnership enrollment process involves the following steps:

  • Step 1: Submit partnership application through our website or direct contact
  • Step 2: Document verification and background check
  • Step 3: Facility/ capability assessment (for vendors and technology partners)
  • Step 4: Land verification and soil testing (for farmer partners)
  • Step 5: Agreement signing and registration
  • Step 6: Onboarding and orientation
  • Step 7: Commencement of partnership activities

7. Payment Terms

Payment terms vary by partnership type:

  • Farmer Partners: Payment within 7-15 days of biomass delivery against valid invoice
  • Vendor Partners: Standard payment terms of 30-45 days from invoice date
  • Technology Partners: As specified in individual technology agreements
  • Strategic Alliances: As mutually agreed in alliance MoU

All payments are subject to applicable tax deductions (TDS) as per Income Tax Act, 1961.

8. Quality Standards and Performance Metrics

All partners are expected to maintain the following standards:

  • Farmer Partners: Napier Grass quality as per defined specifications, timely harvesting, proper land management
  • Vendor Partners: ISO or equivalent quality certification, on-time delivery, warranty compliance
  • Technology Partners: Proven track record, technical support availability, regular updates

Partners will be evaluated quarterly on performance metrics. Consistent underperformance may lead to partnership review or termination.

9. Duration and Termination

Initial Term: Partnership agreements are typically for 3-5 years, depending on partnership type.

Renewal: Agreements may be renewed by mutual consent, subject to satisfactory performance review.

Termination by Partner: Partners may terminate with 30-90 days written notice, depending on agreement terms.

Termination by Atirath Holdings: We reserve the right to terminate partnerships for:

  • Breach of agreement terms
  • Quality or delivery failures
  • Fraudulent or unethical conduct
  • Insolvency or bankruptcy
  • Violation of applicable laws

10. Confidentiality and Data Protection

All partners agree to:

  • Maintain confidentiality of all business information shared during the partnership
  • Not disclose trade secrets, pricing, or operational data to third parties
  • Use partner data only for the intended business purpose
  • Comply with applicable data protection laws
  • Return or destroy confidential information upon termination

11. Code of Conduct

All partners are expected to adhere to our Code of Conduct:

  • Conduct business with integrity and honesty
  • Respect human rights and labor laws
  • Maintain safe working conditions
  • No bribery, corruption, or unethical practices
  • No child or forced labor
  • Environmental responsibility in all operations
  • No discrimination based on caste, creed, gender, or religion

12. Dispute Resolution

Any disputes arising from the partnership shall be resolved through:

  • Step 1 - Informal Discussion: Attempt to resolve amicably through direct communication
  • Step 2 - Formal Mediation: If unresolved, refer to company-appointed mediator
  • Step 3 - Arbitration: Binding arbitration as per Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996
  • Step 4 - Legal Recourse: Courts in Hyderabad, Telangana have exclusive jurisdiction

13. Liability and Indemnification

Each party shall be liable for their own actions and shall indemnify the other against claims arising from their negligence, breach of agreement, or violation of laws. Neither party shall be liable for indirect or consequential damages beyond the value of the agreement.

14. Force Majeure

Neither party shall be liable for delays or failures caused by circumstances beyond reasonable control including natural disasters, government actions, war, pandemic, or labor disputes. Affected party must notify the other within 7 days and take reasonable steps to mitigate impact.

15. Policy Updates and Communication

This policy may be updated from time to time. Partners will be notified of material changes at least 30 days in advance. Continued partnership constitutes acceptance of revised policy. For policy-related queries, contact partnerships@atirathholdings.com.
