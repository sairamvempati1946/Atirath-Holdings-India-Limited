What is SATAT Scheme?

Understanding India's flagship CBG promotion program

Sustainable Alternative Towards Affordable Transportation (SATAT) is a pioneering initiative launched by the Government of India to promote Compressed Biogas (CBG) as a green alternative automotive fuel. The scheme encourages entrepreneurs to set up CBG plants and supply CBG to Oil Marketing Companies (OMCs) for sale as automotive fuel.

Under this scheme, OMCs like Indian Oil, BPCL, and HPCL are inviting Expression of Interest (EOI) from potential entrepreneurs to set up CBG plants. The OMCs will then procure the CBG and retail it through their existing fuel station network.

The scheme is expected to create a robust ecosystem for CBG production, reduce India's import dependency, manage agricultural waste, and create rural employment opportunities.

Eligibility Criteria

Who can apply for setting up a CBG plant under SATAT

For Entities

  • Private companies & LLPs registered in India
  • Partnership firms with minimum 2 partners
  • Proprietorship firms with proven experience
  • Cooperatives and farmer producer organizations
  • Joint ventures with technology partners

Technical Requirements

  • Minimum plant capacity: 10 TPD of CBG
  • Minimum land requirement: 5-10 acres
  • Proximity to feedstock source (within 50 km)
  • Access to water and power supply
  • Environmental clearance as applicable
