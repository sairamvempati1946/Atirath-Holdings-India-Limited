Project Overview

Understanding Compressed Biogas and its importance in India's energy landscape

Compressed Biogas (CBG) is a purified form of biogas that is compressed to high pressure for use as a transportation fuel. It is produced from organic waste materials such as agricultural residue, Napier Grass, cattle dung, press mud, municipal solid waste, and sewage treatment plant waste through a process of anaerobic decomposition.

CBG has similar properties to Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) and can be used as a green renewable fuel for vehicles. It has high methane content (>90%) and calorific value, making it an excellent alternative to fossil fuels.

Our CBG manufacturing plants utilize state-of-the-art technology to convert agricultural biomass into high-quality Compressed Biogas, contributing to India's energy security and reducing carbon emissions.
