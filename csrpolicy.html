CSR Policy

Our commitment to corporate social responsibility and sustainable development

Last Updated: January 1, 2025

1. Preamble

At Atirath Holdings India Limited, we believe that business success and social responsibility are interconnected. Our Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) policy reflects our commitment to creating positive social and environmental impact while pursuing our business objectives. This policy is guided by the Companies Act, 2013 (Schedule VII) and aligns with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

2. CSR Vision

To be a responsible corporate citizen that contributes meaningfully to India's sustainable development by empowering communities, protecting the environment, and creating lasting social value.

3. CSR Mission

To integrate social and environmental considerations into our core business operations and create shared value for all stakeholders through focused interventions in key areas of national priority.

4. CSR Focus Areas

Based on Schedule VII of the Companies Act, 2013, our CSR initiatives focus on:

  • Eradicating hunger, poverty, and malnutrition
  • Promoting education, including special education and employability
  • Gender equality and women empowerment
  • Environmental sustainability and ecological balance
  • Healthcare and sanitation
  • Rural development projects
  • Skill development and livelihood enhancement
  • Swachh Bharat initiatives

5. CSR Committee

The Board of Directors has constituted a CSR Committee comprising:

  • Chairman & Managing Director - Chairperson of CSR Committee
  • Chief Financial Officer - Member
  • Independent Director - Member
  • Head of Sustainability - Convener

The Committee is responsible for formulating and recommending CSR policies, approving projects, monitoring implementation, and ensuring compliance.

6. CSR Budget and Allocation

The company shall spend at least 2% of the average net profits of the immediately preceding three financial years on CSR activities as mandated by the Companies Act, 2013. The annual CSR budget is approved by the Board on the recommendation of the CSR Committee.

7. Implementation Framework

CSR activities are implemented through:

  • Direct implementation by the company
  • Registered trusts/societies established by the company
  • Collaboration with government agencies
  • Partnerships with established NGOs and development organizations
  • Employee volunteering programs

8. Key Initiatives

8.1 Farmer Empowerment Program

Training and supporting farmers for sustainable Napier Grass cultivation with guaranteed buyback, increasing farmer incomes by 200-300%.

8.2 Shiksha - Education for All

Providing scholarships, school infrastructure support, and digital education resources to underprivileged children.

8.3 Arogya - Health Camps

Organizing free health check-up camps, distributing medicines, and promoting hygiene awareness in rural areas.

8.4 Green Drive - Plantation

Massive tree plantation drives to increase green cover and promote biodiversity conservation.

8.5 Nari Shakti - Women Empowerment

Forming self-help groups, providing vocational training, and supporting women entrepreneurs.

8.6 Jal Raksha - Water Conservation

Implementing rainwater harvesting systems and promoting water-efficient agricultural practices.

9. Monitoring and Evaluation

All CSR projects are subject to regular monitoring and evaluation through:

  • Quarterly progress reports to the CSR Committee
  • Annual impact assessment by independent agencies
  • Field visits by CSR Committee members
  • Beneficiary feedback mechanisms
  • Social audits for major projects

10. Reporting and Disclosure

The company shall disclose its CSR activities in the Annual Report, including:

  • Composition of CSR Committee
  • CSR policy
  • Projects undertaken during the financial year
  • Amount spent and unspent with reasons
  • Impact assessment reports

11. Governance and Compliance

The CSR Committee ensures compliance with all applicable laws and regulations. Any surplus arising from CSR activities shall be reinvested into CSR projects and not form part of business profits.

12. Employee Volunteering

We encourage employee participation in CSR activities through paid volunteer leave and recognition programs. Our goal is to achieve at least 80% employee participation in at least one CSR activity annually.

13. Partnerships and Collaboration

We actively seek partnerships with government bodies, NGOs, academic institutions, and other corporations to amplify our social impact. Partners are selected based on their track record, transparency, and alignment with our CSR objectives.

14. Review and Amendment

This policy is reviewed annually by the CSR Committee and the Board. Any amendments require Board approval. The policy is made available on the company website for public reference.
