What is Napier Grass?

Understanding the wonder grass that's transforming Indian agriculture and energy sector

Napier Grass (Pennisetum purpureum), also known as Elephant Grass, is a fast-growing, high-yield perennial grass that has gained significant importance as an ideal feedstock for Compressed Biogas (CBG) production due to its high biomass yield and low input requirements.

Napier Grass can grow up to 4 meters tall and can be harvested 4-5 times per year, yielding 80-100 tons per acre annually. Unlike traditional crops, it requires minimal water, fertilizers, and pesticides, making it an economical and sustainable choice for farmers.

At Atirath Holdings, we promote Napier Grass cultivation as part of our integrated CBG production ecosystem, providing farmers with guaranteed buyback and technical support.

Why Napier Grass?

Key advantages that make Napier Grass the preferred choice for CBG feedstock

Exceptional Biomass Yield

Napier Grass produces 80-100 tons of biomass per acre annually - 10 times more than traditional crops. This high yield ensures consistent and abundant feedstock supply for CBG plants, maximizing production efficiency and profitability.

Low Water & Input Requirements

Unlike traditional crops, Napier Grass requires minimal irrigation and can thrive in semi-arid conditions. It needs 70% less water than sugarcane and doesn't require expensive fertilizers or pesticides, significantly reducing farming costs.

High Methane Potential

Napier Grass has excellent biogas yield with 15-20% methane content, making it ideal for CBG production. Its high cellulose and hemicellulose content ensures efficient anaerobic digestion and maximum gas output.

Low Cost, High Return

With minimal input costs and multiple harvests per year, Napier Grass offers farmers a profit margin of 100-150% compared to traditional crops. The guaranteed buyback from Atirath Holdings ensures stable income.

Cultivation Process

Simple 5-step flowchart for successful Napier Grass farming
1
Land Preparation
Plow and level the field to ensure proper drainage. Remove weeds and add organic manure.
2
Planting
Use stem cuttings or root slips planted at 1m x 1m spacing during monsoon season.
3
Irrigation & Care
Initial watering is crucial. Apply organic fertilizer after each harvest.
4
First Harvest
Ready in 90 days when height reaches 1-1.5 meters. Cut at 5-10 cm above ground.
5
Regular Harvest
Subsequent harvests every 45-60 days. 4-5 harvests annually for 5-7 years.
